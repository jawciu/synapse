I've been thinking about why I catastrophize so much and I think I learned it from my mum. She was always preparing for the worst — "what if the car breaks down," "what if you fail the exam," "what if something happens to dad on his way home." Her love came wrapped in worry. I grew up believing the world was fundamentally unsafe and that the only responsible thing to do was anticipate every disaster. Now I'm 32 and I lie awake on Sunday nights running through worst-case scenarios for Monday. It's like I inherited her anxiety as a way of feeling close to her. If I stop worrying, am I betraying her somehow? That sounds irrational but it feels true in my body.
