My partner and I had a fight and I did the thing again — completely shut down. She was trying to talk about something that bothered her and I just went quiet, monosyllabic, emotionally gone. She got frustrated and said "it's like talking to a wall." She's right. I know I do this. When emotions get intense in relationships, something in me just leaves. I think it's connected to watching my parents. Their fights were loud and scary — mum crying, dad slamming doors. I learned that emotional intensity is dangerous. So now, the second a conversation gets heated, a part of me pulls the emergency brake. I withdraw to feel safe but it pushes away the people I love most. My partner isn't my parents. This fight wasn't dangerous. But my body doesn't know the difference yet.
