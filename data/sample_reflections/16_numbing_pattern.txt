I noticed something uncomfortable today. When I got that critical email from my manager, I didn't react outwardly — I just went completely numb. Then I spent the next three hours mindlessly scrolling my phone, ordered food I didn't really want, and watched YouTube until 1am. It's like there's a part of me that shuts everything down the second real feelings start to surface. I've done this my whole life. When my parents argued when I was a kid, I'd go to my room and lose myself in video games for hours. I learned to disconnect. The numbing kept me safe back then but now it's stealing whole evenings from me. By the time I "come back" I feel hollow and the original feeling — the hurt from the email — is still there underneath, unprocessed.
