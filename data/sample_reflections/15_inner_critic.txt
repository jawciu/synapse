The voice in my head that tells me I'm not good enough — I'm starting to realize it sounds exactly like my dad. Not the words maybe, but the tone. Dismissive, impatient, like I should already know better. When I make a mistake at work, this voice kicks in immediately: "you're going to get found out," "everyone else manages this easily," "you're fundamentally broken." I used to think this voice WAS me. But lately I'm wondering if it's more like a guard — something that developed to protect me from the pain of being criticized by someone else. If I criticize myself first, no one else's words can hurt as much. The problem is this inner critic never takes a day off. It's exhausting being at war with yourself. I wonder what would happen if I could thank it for trying to protect me, and ask it to step back.
