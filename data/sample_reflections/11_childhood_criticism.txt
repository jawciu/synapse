Something came up in therapy today that shook me. We were talking about why I freeze when I get feedback at work, and my therapist asked when I first remember feeling that way. Immediately I was back in the kitchen, age 8, showing my dad a drawing I'd made. He barely looked at it and said "that's nice but shouldn't you be doing your homework?" I remember the feeling — like I'd offered something precious and it was dismissed. I think I decided right then that nothing I create is good enough unless it's productive or "useful." That's probably why I spend 4 hours perfecting an email. There's this small part of me that's still that kid holding up a drawing, terrified it'll be dismissed again.
