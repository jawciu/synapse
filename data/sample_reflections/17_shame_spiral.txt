Something small happened today and it unlocked this avalanche of shame. I mispronounced a word in a meeting and someone corrected me. Objectively, nothing. But I felt my face burn and suddenly I was flooded with memories — being laughed at in year 6 for getting an answer wrong, my dad rolling his eyes when I didn't understand his instructions, that time at university when a professor said my question was "basic." It's like there's a vault inside me full of every moment I've ever felt stupid, and one small key opens the whole thing. The shame isn't proportional to what happened. It's ancient. I carry decades of "you're not smart enough" moments stacked on top of each other, and they all activate at once. I just want to hide when that happens. I cancelled my after-work plans and went straight home.
